Choice - Getting Screened

  • First, read the following sentences and answer “Yes” or “No” for each one, depending on whether the sentence sounds like you or not.
  • Then, look at all the sentences that you answered “Yes”.
  • If you have more “Yes” than “No” answers you may lean more toward getting screened.

Men who have made these statements often decide to get screened.
Because the DRE and PSA are both medical exams available to diagnose prostate problems, I prefer to have these exams routinely during my wellness visits.
Because prostate cancer risk increases with age and can become a very serious disease if not discovered early, I prefer to be screened regularly.
I understand that screening may or may not help to save my life. Even so, I prefer to get screened because I think it is better to be safe than sorry.
I understand that prostate cancer may never cause me problems. Even then, I prefer to be screened at this time so I can deal with the consequences of prostate cancer as early as possible.
Screening may lead to being diagnosed with prostate cancer. I will need to accept living with untreated cancer, or the side effects of treatment. Still, I would prefer to routinely have screenings for the possibility of more treatment options and a longer life.

Discussing Results

If your PSA test of DRE is abnormal, your doctor may suggest a repeat PSA test.
  • This will help to see if the earlier test was accurate
  • Your doctor may test for the level of “free” PSA
  • A repeat PSA test can also determine if the PSA level changes over time. Doctors call this the PSA velocity.
  • Also, your doctor may test PSA density, or the size of your prostate and how it compares to your PSA number.
“Free” versus “Attached” PSA

PSA circulates in the blood in 2 forms: free or attached to a protein molecule:

  • A “free” PSA test only measures the amount of PSA that is floating freely in the bloodstream.
  • An “attached” PSA test only measures the amount of PSA that is attached (bound) to a protein molecule.

In healthy prostates there is more of the “free” form of PSA. In prostates with cancer, there is more of the “attached” form of PSA.

Many doctors use the measure of “free” PSA to help them interpret a man’s PSA level.

PSA Velocity
  • A PSA velocity is the change in PSA levels over time.
  • A sharp rise in the PSA level raises the suspicion of cancer.
Density PSA
  • PSA levels are higher in men with larger prostate glands.
  • The doctor measures the volume (size) of the prostate gland with and divides the PSA number by the prostate volume.
  • A higher PSA density indicates a greater likelihood of cancer.

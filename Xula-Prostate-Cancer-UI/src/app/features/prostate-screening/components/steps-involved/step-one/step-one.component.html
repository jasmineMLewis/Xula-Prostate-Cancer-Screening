Step 1: Get Tested

Prostate Test

As you learned before, there are two tests used for screening for prostate cancer:

  • The Prostate Specific Antigen test or PSA (blood) test
  • The Digital Rectal Examination or the DRE (finger) exam

Which Test

No test is right all the time. The PSA test is better at finding small cancers, or those that cannot be felt by the DRE. The DRE can sometimes help find cancers in men with normal PSA levels.

Some doctors order both tests at the same time, while other doctors order the PSA and, depending on results, perform the DRE.

Prostate Exams: PSA & DRE

PSA: PSA stands for Prostate Specific Antigen. PSA is a substance (protein) made by the prostate gland and released into the blood. The PSA test measures the level of PSA in the blood to determine if follow up testing is needed. *

DRE: DRE stands for Digital Rectal Exam. A doctor inserts a gloved and lubricated finger into the rectum. This allows the doctor to feel the back portion of the prostate to check for hard or lumpy areas that require follow-up testing.*

* See Module 1, “The Importance of the Prostate”, for additional information.

Biopsy

  • A biopsy is the removal of tiny samples of tissue, that are examined under a microscope to find out if there are cancer cells in the sample.
  • The biopsy is usually performed in the urologist's office. A needle is used to collect the samples during the TRUS or MRI tests.
  • If the biopsy results are normal: You probably do not have cancer. If the biopsy results are abnormal: You may have cancer. Although biopsies are not 100% accurate, they are the best method we have today of finding out if cancer is present.

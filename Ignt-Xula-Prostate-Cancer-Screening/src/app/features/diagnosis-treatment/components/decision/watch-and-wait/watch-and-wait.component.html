Watch and Wait

Some men choose to watch if their symptoms increase or cancer grows.

  • The cancer is closely watched by having the PSA and/or DRE tests regularly, and undergoing additional biopsies, if needed.
  • The cancer is treated only if it shows signs of growing fast or when it causes symptoms.
  • Main risks of watching and waiting:
    • The cancer may spread, making it more difficult to treat later.
    • Treatment options may be limited once the cancer has spread.
    • The cancer could become fatal.
PSA, DRE, & Biopsy

PSA: PSA stands for Prostate Specific Antigen. PSA is a substance (protein) made by the prostate gland and released into the blood. The PSA test measures the level of PSA in the blood.*

DRE: DRE stands for Digital Rectal Exam. A doctor inserts a gloved and lubricated finger into the rectum. This allows the doctor to feel the back portion of the prostate to check for hard or lumpy areas.*

Biopsy: The removal of a sample of tissue, which is then examined under a microscope to check for cancerous changes.

* See Module 1, “The Importance of the Prostate”, for additional information.

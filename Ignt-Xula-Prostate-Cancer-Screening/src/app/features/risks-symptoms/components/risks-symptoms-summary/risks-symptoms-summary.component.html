Summary of Module 2

We just learned that:

  • Age, family history, and race are important risks factors for prostate cancer.
  • There are men who have prostate cancer and have no symptoms at all. Also, there are men who have prostate-related symptoms and do NOT have cancer.
  • Generally, urination problems and blood in the urine are the most common symptoms of prostate cancer.
  • Although having those symptoms does not mean you have prostate cancer, it is highly recommended to visit the doctor.

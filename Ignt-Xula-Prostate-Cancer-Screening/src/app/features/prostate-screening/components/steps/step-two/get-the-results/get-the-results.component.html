  • There is not full agreement about the PSA level that indicates when follow-up testing is needed.
  • In general, if your PSA level is higher than 4.0 ng/mL (nanograms per milliliter), it is recommended that you discuss the next steps with your doctor.
  • For some men, higher PSA values may be considered normal. However, there are many other factors such as race, age, and family history to consider when looking at the PSA ranges and before taking any action.

What if the screening results are abnormal?

Your doctor will review with you different reasons that a test may be abnormal:
  • The results may be wrong
  • The abnormal results may be due to non-cancerous conditions such as an enlarged prostate (benign prostatic hyperplasia, or BPH) or an inflamed or infected prostate (prostatitis).
  • You may have prostate cancer
Regardless of the reasons, it will be necessary to have more tests to confirm results.
Abnormal Conditions

BPH: BPH stands for benign prostatic hyperplasia, or an enlarged prostate. A prostate health problem that often occurs as men age.*

Prostatitis: Inflammation of the prostate. A common prostate health problem that is usually caused by an infection.*

Prostate Cancer: Begins when cells in the prostate gland start to grow out of control and form a cancerous tumor. **


* See Module 1, “The Importance of the Prostate”, for additional information.

** See Module 1, “The Importance of the Prostate”, and Module 2, “Risks and Symptoms”, for additional information.

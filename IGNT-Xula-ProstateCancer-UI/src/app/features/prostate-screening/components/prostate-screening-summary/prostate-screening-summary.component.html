Summary of Module 3

We just learned that:

  • Cancer screening looks for cancer before a person has any symptoms. It helps to find cancer at an early stage, when it is easier to treat.
  • The most common tests for prostate cancer screening are the PSA test (blood test) and the DRE (finger) exam.
  • Since for some men a higher PSA level may be normal, there is not full agreement about what PSA level indicates the need for further testing.
  • For men who choose to have the PSA test, getting normal results may give them peace of mind. However, men who receive abnormal results may be worried that they may have prostate cancer. They will need to undergo additional follow-up testing to know the diagnosis.
  • Ultimately, every man will have to make the best decision for their circumstances about whether to be screened for prostate cancer.

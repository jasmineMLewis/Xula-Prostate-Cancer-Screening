Choice - Not Getting Screened

  • Now, read the following sentences and answer “Yes” or “No” for each one, depending on whether the sentence sounds like you or not.
  • Then, look at all the sentences that you answered “Yes”.
  • If you have more “Yes” than “No” answers you may lean more toward NOT getting screened.

Men who have made these statements often decide NOT to get screened.
Although DRE and PSA are both medical exams available to identify prostate problems, I prefer to wait to have these tests later or to make a decision later about whether to have these tests.
If I am not at high risk for prostate cancer, it does not make sense to me to be screened for prostate cancer.
I understand that screening may or may not help to save my life. I also understand that screening may give a false result. I think I am better off not getting screened.
Since prostate cancer may never cause me problems, I think I am better off not getting screened.
Screening may lead to being diagnosed with prostate cancer. I will need to accept living with untreated cancer, or the side effects of treatment. These consequences are not worth the possibility of more treatment options and a longer life. Currently, I prefer not to be screened.
